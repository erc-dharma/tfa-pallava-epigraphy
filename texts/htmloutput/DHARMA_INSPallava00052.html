Translation by Emmanuel Francis

The great carpenter Kevātaṉ.

Commentary

This inscription is part of a series of names found on a group of rocks. See also INSPallava00514-00520. Six names (INSPallava00052, 00514-00517, 00520 ) are found on the rock locally known as noṇṭi vīrappaṉ kutiraitoṭṭi (“the watering place of the horse of Noṇṭi Vīrappaṉ”), one on a second rock nearby (INSPallava00518), one on a third rock in the same place (INSPallava00519).

See "The following seven labels are engraved in archaic Tamil and Grantha characters on a group of rocks, one of which, on account of a natural cavity in it, is locally known as ’Noṇḍi Vīrappaṉ Kudiraitoṭṭi’. The names Kevadaperundachchaṉ and Kollaṉ Śēmagaṉ found in this place indicate that the persons bearing these names belonged to the artisan class. One of the labels also gives the name Guṇamallaḥ."
