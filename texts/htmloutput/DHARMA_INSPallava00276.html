Translation by Emmanuel Francis

1-2Tirthy-third [year] of the victorious king SiṃhaviṣṇuvarmanCiṅkaviṇṇaparumaṉ in the original Tamil..

2-11[This is] the stone of the son of the general ēṉāti Kantaparupu / of KantaparupuThat is, Kantaparumaṉ, according to TVMMahalingam 1988., he who fell paṭṭāṉ fighting eṟintu when Pacirappaṇṇaṉ Kuṟaṭṭātaṉ, who was ruling over Toppuravaru-p-pāṭi [and who was] a servant of Viṇṇaṉṉār, took [...] ... varamu against mēlLiterally “upon”. the people makkaḷ of Kaṅkati-AraicaruThat is, the Gaṅga king..

Notes

↑1. Ciṅkaviṇṇaparumaṉ in the original Tamil.
↑2. That is, Kantaparumaṉ, according to TVMMahalingam 1988.
↑3. Literally “upon”.
↑4. That is, the Gaṅga king.
