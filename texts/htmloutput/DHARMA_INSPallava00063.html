Translation by Emmanuel Francis

May Rājasiṃha (the lion among kings) Raṇajaya (the victorious one among kings) Śrībhara (the Fortune-bearer) Citrakārmuka (he whose quiver is a wonder) Ekavīra (the unique hero) Śivacūḍāmaṇi (he who has Śiva as diadem) protect the earth for long.

Translation into French by Sylvain Brocquet

Puisse Rājasiṃha, le vainqueur au combat,
Celui qui apporte la prospérité, celui qui possède un arc resplendissant,
Le héros unique, longtemps protéger la terre,
Lui qui porte Śiva comme diadème !

Commentary

The same verse is found in two other inscriptions of Narasiṃhavarman II. See INSPallava00054 (stanza 12) and INSPallava00060 (stanza 3).
