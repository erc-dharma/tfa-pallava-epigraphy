Translation by Emmanuel Francis

1-2First year of the glorious Mahārāja Parameśvaravarman.

3-4The temple that Kumāraṉ, nephew (sister’s son) of Cōmāciyār in (that is, from) Ālavāy, [one] among the tūṇaṅ-kiḷavars [...] has built.

5-6He who does ((c)eytār) harm (aḻivu) [...] will be considered like (ēṟkka) ... (taṭumāv).

Commentary

1-2talaitt-(ā)vatu. See EI, p. 199 fn3.

2ālavāyil. Desai 1957–1958 connects this word with eṭuppitta (lines 3-4), suggesting that Ālavāy is the ancient name of Ciṟṟampākkam/Teṉkāraṇai. We follow Sircar’s opinion (editor’s note to Desai 1957–1958) that Ālavāy is the residence of Cōmāciyār. So does Mahalingam 1988: 147, note 1.

2-3cōmāciyāru. See EI, p. 200 fn1.

3 maru-maḵaṉ. This term means more precisely “sister’s son” in the present case.
