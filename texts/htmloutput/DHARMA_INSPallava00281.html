Translation by Emmanuel Francis

1-3Tirthy-second [year] of the victorious king Mahendravarman↓1.

3-6[This is] the stone [of] Viṉṟaṇ (Va)ṭukaṉ, the servant of Poṟmōtaṉār↓2, he who fell fighting (kutti↓3) with a tiger.

Notes

↑1. Mayēntiraparumaṉ in the original Tamil.
↑2. Cf. Poṟmōtaṉṉār in DHARMA_INSPallava00280, same findspot, king, and year.
↑3. Literally “piercing, striking”.
