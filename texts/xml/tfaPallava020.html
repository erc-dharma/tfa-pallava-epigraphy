Translation

Prosperity! The king (who wore) a beautiful garland of toṇḍai, Narēndra-Pōttaraiyaṉ, whose cruel bow bristled with arrows, made with great joy in the south of Veṇbeṭṭu (this temple) named Śatrumallēśvarālaya, to be the residence of Hara (Śiva).

Brahmamaṅgalavaṉ Śellaṉ Śivadāsa of this village composed this.

Translation

Fortune ! Le roi à la belle guirlande de toṇṭai (ou: Le glorieux roi à la belle guirlande de toṇṭai), le roi-Pallava (ou: le roi-taureau) (pōtt-araiyaṉ) Narendra, au sud de Veṇpēṭu (le hameau blanc), avec grande joie, a fait en sorte (kaṇṭāṉ, littéralement: a supervisé) qu’il y ait pour Araṉ (kt. Hara, i.e Śiva) un lieu (iṭam) nommé (eṉṟu) demeure du Seigneur de Catturumalla (Skt. Śatrumalla), dont l’arc cruel/furieux regorge de flèches.

Ce qu’a composé (colliyatu, littéralement: ce qu'a dit) Brammamaṅkalavaṉ Cellaṉ Civadāsan (Skt. Śivadāsa) de ce village.

Commentary

To be read with the Sanskrit verse,which is outside the cave.

Identification of Narēntirappōttaraiyaṉ to be discussed.

śrī = benediction or part of a compound ("glorious king ...)?

(10) āṅku: either "there," that is, "here," that is in Veṇpēṭu, or "thus," concluding the verse, that is, "so was it done"
