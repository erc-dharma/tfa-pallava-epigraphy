Apparatus

Translation

In the fiftieth (year) of Nandippôttaraśar,― Nâraṇaṉ (Nârâyaṇa), the son of Maruttuvar ([fn4]) of Pugaḻâlaimaṅgalam, caused to be engraved an image of Poṉṉiyakkiyâr, along with [fn5] the preceptor (gurava) [fn6] Nâgaṇandi (Nâganandin). [fn4] The word maruttuvaṉ means ‘a physician.’ In the present case it seems to be the proper name of a person. [fn5] Literally: “while there is present.”[fn6] On this word, which appears to be derived fro the honorific plural of guru, see south-Indian Inscriptions, Vol. II. p. 251, note 3.

Translation

Fiftieth [year] of Nantippōttaracar (i.e. Nandippōtaraiyar, Nandivarman).

Nāraṇaṉ (i.e. Nārāyaṇa), son of Maruttuvar of Pukaḻālaimaṅkalam, caused to be engraved [this] image of Poṉṉiyakkiyār accompanied with(irukka) the Preceptor Nākaṇandi.

Commentary

(1) nāka(ṇa)ndi-kura(var)
(2) (Iru)kka Literally: while the Preceptor Nākaṇandi is present.
